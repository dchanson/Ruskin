“Aix la Chapelle” [prose]
“atreaty” (MS VIII)—In manuscript, the two words are run together.


Pause–period (MS MS VIII)—See Editorial and Encoding Rationale and Methodology: Element, Attribute, and Value Usage—Commas, Periods, and Other Punctuation.


“orso” (MS VIII)—The s in so is written over top of the tail of the r of or. Followed by a terminal comma (see Editorial and Encoding Rationale and Methodology: Element, Attribute, and Value Usage—Commas, Periods, and Other Punctuation.


Terminal comma (MS VIII)—See Editorial and Encoding Rationale and Methodology: Element, Attribute, and Value Usage—Commas, Periods, and Other Punctuation.


“Twili” (MS VIII)—Ruskin perhaps started to write Twilight.


Justification mark (MS IX)—See Editorial and Encoding Rationale and Methodology: Element, Attribute, and Value Usage—Justification, Runover, and Word Division.


“Return,d” (MS IX)—In MS VIII, Ruskin correctly uses an apostrophe to form the contraction.

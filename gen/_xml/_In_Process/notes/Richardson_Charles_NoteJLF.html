Charles Richardson
Charles Thomas Richardson (1811–34)
Cousin of John Ruskin, a son of Margaret Ruskinʼs sister, Bridget, and her husband George Richardson, a baker. The Richardsons lived in the suburb of Croydon, near the Kingʼs Head, the tavern operated by Margaret and Bridgetʼs mother, Margaret Cock, and the sistersʼ birthplace. Margaret took John from a very young age to visit his Croydon cousins, though he was distanced from the family, being kept on walks or in his room, except for visits by Charles and his sister Mary (Margaret to John James Ruskin, 30 January 1822, in Burd, ed., Ruskin Family Letters, 108).
By October 1829, Charles was employed by the publisher, Smith, Elder, for at this time he presented John with a copy of Friendshipʼs Offering, the “Literary Album and Annual Remembrancer” that the firm had recently taken over from its initial publisher. The editor was Thomas Pringle (1789–1834), who was possibly introduced to the Ruskin family through the Smith, Elder connection. According to Praeterita, Charles decided to emigrate to Australia to join his brother, ???, because advancement at the publishing firm was unlikely. Charles was drowned while the ship remained anchored off Cowes in January 1834 (see Ruskin, Works, 35: 135-37). One wonders whether Pringle—whose experience had been mixed as an immigrant to South Africa, and who coincidentally died later in the same year—had encouraged the move.

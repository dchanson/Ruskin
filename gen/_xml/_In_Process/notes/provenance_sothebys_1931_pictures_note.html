Sothebyʼs Sale of Ruskin Pictures and Drawings, 1931

On 20 May 1931, Sotheby & Co. held a sale of paintings and drawings by or belonging to Ruskin, two days following the Sothebyʼs Sale of Ruskin Manuscripts and Library, 1931. As described in the title of the 1931 catalogue, the sale represented The Collection of Pictures and Drawings, Formerly the Property of the Late Professor John Ruskin, Removed from Brantwood, Coniston (Sold by Order of the Trustees), and the Property of the Late Joseph Arthur Palliser‐Severn, R.I., R.O.I., Removed from 9, Warwick Square, London, S.W..
On 21 May, the day following the sale, the B. F. Stevens & Brown firm cabled Goodspeed that they had "secured cream collection comprising about seventy framed items". The firm specified that they "bought all lots specially wanted except onefourfour [lot 144]". The especially desired items included George Richmond's two portraits of Ruskin--the 1857 chalk head portrait (lot 29) for £33, and the 1842 full-length portrait (lot 30) for £82. The total was 25 purchases for a little over £700 (telegram, B. F. Stevens & Brown to Goodspeed, 21 May 1931, in ; and see Sotheby & Co., Catalogue of the Collection of Pictures and Drawings . . . of the Late Professor John Ruskin).

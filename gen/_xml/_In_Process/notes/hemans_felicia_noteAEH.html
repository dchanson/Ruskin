Felicia Hemans (1793–1835)

Hemansʼs poem, “The Sound of the Sea” (1826), is one of the very few works by a writer other than Ruskin himself to be fair-copied in the early manuscripts.
Hemansʼs career straddled the waning world of literary patronage and the emerging world of literary publishing, in which authors vied with publishers for control. In 1821, she received the first prize awarded by the Royal Society of Literture, for her poem on Dartmoor (Wolfson, Hemans, 485–86; Merriam, Edward Moxon, 19).

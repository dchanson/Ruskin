After Ruskinʼs death, by terms of the will, Wedderburn was named literary trustee along with Charles Eliot Norton and Joan Severn. He also received a third of the profits from books (subsequently?) edited or revised by him, along with £100 a year to supervise sales. In 1900, on Ruskinʼs death, Wedderburn advised Allen and the trustees that, to maintain copyright, they should issue new editions of Ruskinʼs works incorporating formerly unpublished material.
Wedderburn appears to have been responsible for arranging and binding Ruskinʼs manuscripts after 1900, once work began on the Library Edition, and he prepared the catalog of manuscripts for the bibliography volume (vol. 38).

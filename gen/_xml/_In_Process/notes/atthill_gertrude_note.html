Gertrude Atthill

As granddaughter of Ruskinʼs cousin, Mary Richardson, Atthill both inherited and purchased a collection related to Ruskin family history. Items from the early years included Mary Richardsonʼs diaries of 1833 and 1840–41 (now at the Huntington Library ), and one of her sketchbooks (now at Brantwood); and the portraits of Margaret Ruskin, by James Northcote, and of John James Ruskin, by Watson (both now at Brantwood). Dearden speculated that all the Ruskin drawings listed in the Catalogue of Drawings (Works 38:) as owned by J. J. Bolding (Atthillʼs father) belonged to her, as well, but these had disappeared (see Dearden, “Mrs Gertrude Atthill”).

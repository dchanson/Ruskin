“Harry took one of his lesson books and made Lucy learn the following constellations with the number of stars” (MS IIIA)—In Frank: A Sequel to Frank in Early Lessons by Maria Edgeworth, Frank “began counting to [his cousin] Mary all the stars, whose names he had lately learned”. In Frankʼs case, he brings shame on himself for showing off, “talking of . . . [the stars] as if they were all his own familiar acquaintance, and had scarcely been heard of by any body else in the world”. While an episode like this in Edgeworthʼs Early Lessons series may in part be behind the “lesson book” that Ruskinʼs Harry invokes to assign Lucy her task, these tales do not contain data of the kind that Ruskin sourced to compile lists of constellation names and corresponding numbers in his third volume of “Harry and Lucy Concluded”.

"Title of Work"
“Blenheim“ (MS III)—The village of Blenheim on the Danube, where, on 13 August 1704, the English defeated French and Bavarian forces, leading ultimately to the Treaty of Utrecht in 1713 that secured British colonial expansion. The victory dealt a blow to the cause of the young Pretender, recognized by France, and the increased British trading strength enabled Parliament to dictate terms to the Scots in the 1707 Act of Union.


“Waterloo“ (MS III)—The decisive British victory over Napoleon.


“Marlborough“ (MS III)—John Churchill, earl of Marlborough, who commanded the forces at Blenheim. As a result of this and other victories, he was rewarded with the title of duke and Blenheim Palace, designed by John Vanbrugh.

“Bannockburn“ (MS III)—The boggy ground where, in 1314, the army of Edward II, successor to Edward I, “the Hammer of the Scots,” was trapped and massacred by Scottish forces under Robert Bruce.

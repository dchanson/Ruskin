Title
Ruskin entitled the collection “Poetry,” just as he had headed the MS I Poetry Anthology as “Poetry”.

Contents
The anthology contains the following poems, in order of their appearance:
  • “The Constellations: Northern, Some of the Zodiac, and Some of the Southern”;
  • “Time: Blank Verse”;
  • “The Sun”;
  • “Glen of Glenfarg” (“Papa how pretty those icicles are”);
  • “The Ship” [1827];
  • “The British Battles.”
Date
Ruskin compiled this anthology in MS III in 1828, sometime after New Yearʼs Day, making it the second anthology in this Red Book, after Poetry Descriptive (1827) (see MS III: Discussion; see also MS III Third Poetry Anthology).
Compilation
A drawing, Ship and House, [Miscellaneous Drawing, MS III], falls within the anthology, and probably predates it. Ruskin probably intended the drawing to illustrate “Harry and Lucy,” Vol. 2, but, ending the narrative earlier than expected, wrapped the drawing round by “Time: Blank Verse.” Another drawing, “Ragland Castle When Newly Built,” [Miscellaneous Drawing, MS III], forms the terminus of the anthology and, again, probably predates it, but this drawing can be dated less securely (see MS III: Discussion; MS III Drawings).
Themes
See Poetry Descriptive for W. G. Collingwoodʼs assumption that this collection along with the second form a single anthology. Of the second anthology, however, only “Glen of Glenfarg” (“Papa how pretty those icicles are”) is “descriptive” in the topographical sense of the earlier collection, the second being dominated more by catalog poetry and New Yearʼs poetry.
